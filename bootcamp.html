What we do in class
  • Browse the placement list on this site — explore what's available across all 7 career clusters

  • Match your skills from your resume to the placements you're interested in

Employer research — know before you go
  • Before applying: look up their website, LinkedIn, and Google reviews. What do they do? Who works there? Did they recently expand or hire?
  • Goal: walk into your interview able to say "I noticed you just opened a second location" or "I saw your team focuses heavily on youth programs" — not "so what do you guys do again?"
After you apply
  • Send a follow-up email 5–7 business days after applying if you haven't heard back — brief, professional, confirms your interest
  • Send a thank-you email within 24 hours of any interview — one paragraph, reference something specific from the conversation, confirm you want the role
  • Both emails go in your portfolio — they're evidence of professional communication, not just formalities

3
Mock Interview
Peer speed interviews · Formal mock · Real feedback · Question bank
~90 min
To Do ▾
Step 1 — Peer speed interviews (whole class)
  • Class splits in half: one side interviews, the other answers. Each pair gets 2–3 questions from the interview question bank, then rotate down the line.
  • After 30 minutes, switch sides — the interviewers become interviewees. Everyone practices both roles.
  • Interviewees don't know which questions are coming. That's the point — you get comfortable answering live, unrehearsed, for a rotating set of people.
Step 2 — Formal mock interview (community partner)
  • 1-on-1 with a business partner, chamber member, or administrator — ideally someone you don't know well
  • Your interviewer uses a structured feedback form. You receive written feedback immediately after — specific, honest, actionable.
  • Goal is two interviews each during this block. When you're not in the chair, review the feedback from your last one.
  • No one gets a perfect mock interview. That's the point. You find out what to fix before the real one.

1
Workplace Dilemma Stations
15 dilemmas · Carousel stations · Career clusters · Class debrief
~90 min
To Do ▾
What we do in class
  • Carousel activity: 15 dilemma stations set up around the room. Small groups rotate every few minutes, read the scenario, and discuss what they'd do.
  • Scenarios span all 7 career clusters — a healthcare confidentiality situation, a manufacturing safety shortcut, a supervisor asking something that feels wrong in an education setting, a customer conflict in retail, and more
  • After rotating, the class picks 2–3 scenarios to debrief together — groups share what they decided and why
  • You'll disagree with each other. That's the activity. There's often no single right answer — what matters is how you think it through.
  • If you were absent: all 15 dilemma scenarios are linked here so you can review them and mark complete

What this means

  • Your employer may also have their own onboarding paperwork — especially if you're getting paid. This can include tax forms, direct deposit info, and ID verification. Sit down with a parent or guardian to complete these. Don't guess on legal documents.

Required Before Day 1 — No Exceptions
Your Training Agreement must be signed by all four parties — you, a parent or guardian, your placement employer, and your teacher — and returned before your first day at your placement.
